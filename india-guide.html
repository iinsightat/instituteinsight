India Study Guide

This guide provides admission tips, top universities, scholarships, and practical advice for students planning to study in India—one of the world's largest and fastest-growing education systems.

Why Study in India?
  • Home to globally recognized institutes (IITs, IIMs, IISc, NITs, AIIMS)
  • Affordable tuition fees and low living costs
  • Rich cultural heritage and student diversity
  • Strong science, engineering, business, and medical programs
  • English-medium education available in most programs
  • Expanding international student network and research opportunities
Top Indian Institutes
  • IIT Bombay, IIT Delhi, IIT Madras, IIT Kharagpur, IIT Kanpur
  • Indian Institute of Science (IISc), NITs, IIITs, AIIMS (medical), IIMs (business)
  • University of Delhi, Jawaharlal Nehru University, University of Hyderabad
  • See detailed lists on National Institutes
Eligibility & Application Process
  • Academic transcripts/marksheets (class 12th, undergraduate if applying for PG)
  • Entrance exams:
    • Engineering: JEE Main/Advanced
    • Medical: NEET
    • Business: CAT, XAT, etc.
    • General: CUET (for central university admissions), institute entrance tests
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP), Letters of Recommendation (for PG, research)
  • Apply online at the respective institute portal or via national application systems
  • For international students: Check embassy visa requirements and eligibility norms
Key Scholarships
  • National Scholarships Portal (Apply here)
  • Institute-specific scholarships (check college websites)
  • AICTE, UGC, DST, DBT scholarships for research and technical students
  • State government schemes (varies by state)
  • International scholarships for inbound students (SAARC, ICCR, etc.)
  • See full list: InstituteInsight
Student Life & Tips
  • Most campuses have hostels/PGs; private rental and homestays also available
  • Join college clubs, fests, and student organizations
  • Get a local SIM card, medical insurance, and emergency contacts
  • Learn basic Hindi/English phrases for daily life
  • Cities like Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore are major education + cultural hubs
  • Budget: INR 80,000–2,00,000/year for living costs (varies by city and type)
Frequently Asked Questions
  • Can international students apply?
    Yes, most central and state universities admit international students; check specific eligibility and visa rules.
  • Are degrees recognized globally?
    Degrees from top Indian institutes are recognized worldwide; check international equivalence for your program.
  • Do I need to know Hindi?
    No, but knowing basic Hindi is helpful. Most urban and campus environments use English for academics and communications.
  • What is the exam calendar?
    Entrance exams are generally held March–June, admissions July–September.
