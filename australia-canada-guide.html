Australia & Canada Study Guide

This guide provides practical advice, eligibility requirements, featured universities, scholarships, and student life essentials for those planning to study in Australia or Canada.

Why Study in Australia or Canada?
  • World-class universities and research institutes
  • Strong multicultural communities and global student networks
  • English medium education throughout
  • Post-study work opportunities and safe environments
  • Extensive scholarship and financial aid options
  • Quality of life, outdoor lifestyle, and cultural diversity
Popular Universities
  • Australia: University of Melbourne, Australian National University, UNSW Sydney, University of Queensland, Monash University
  • Canada: University of Toronto, University of British Columbia, McGill University, University of Waterloo, McMaster University
Eligibility & Application Process
  • Academic transcripts (recent school/college marksheets)
  • Language proficiency (IELTS, TOEFL for both countries)
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP) and Letters of Recommendation
  • Apply via institute's admission portal or through official country guidelines
  • Visa requirements: Australia (Subclass 500), Canada (Study Permit)
Scholarships & Financial Aid
  • Australia Awards Scholarships (govt.) Apply
  • University-specific scholarships (see university websites)
  • Canadian Commonwealth Scholarship Program Apply
  • Vanier Canada Graduate Scholarships
  • Other provincial and private scholarships (see relevant institution pages)
Student Life & Tips
  • Stay in university hostels, PGs, shared apartments, or homestays
  • Explore campus support groups and international student offices
  • Get local health insurance and emergency contacts
  • Budget for living costs (rent, food, transport, books, activities)
  • Respect local laws, culture, and participate in community events
Frequently Asked Questions
  • Can I work while studying?
    Yes. Both countries allow part-time work on student visas.
  • Are scholarships open to international students?
    Yes. Most national, university, and some provincial scholarships are open to international students.
  • Are IELTS/TOEFL necessary for English-medium applicants?
    Usually yes, but waivers exist for prior English education or specific institution policies.
  • What's the average living cost?
    Australia: AUD 22,000/year; Canada: CAD 15,000–20,000/year (varies by city and lifestyle).
