Europe & Asia Study Guide

This guide covers admission advice, top universities, scholarships, and life tips for students heading to Europe or Asia for higher education. Find practical advice, eligibility, and trusted resources for regions like Germany, UK, France, Singapore, Hong Kong, Japan, and more.

Why Study in Europe or Asia?
  • Diverse academic programs and research opportunities
  • Globally ranked universities
  • Historic cities, culture, and international exposure
  • Affordable or free education options (Europe: Germany, France, etc.)
  • World-class scientific, engineering, and business institutes
  • English-taught degrees available in most countries
Popular Universities
  • Europe: University of Oxford (UK), ETH Zurich (Switzerland), University of Cambridge (UK), Sorbonne University (France), Technical University of Munich (Germany)
  • Asia: National University of Singapore, University of Tokyo (Japan), Tsinghua University (China), University of Hong Kong, Indian Institute of Science (IISc), IITs (India)
Eligibility & Application Process
  • Academic transcripts and certificates (school/college records)
  • Language requirements:
    • Europe: IELTS/TOEFL for English programs; some countries require additional local language proficiency (German, French, etc.)
    • Asia: IELTS/TOEFL/Mandarin (China); other local requirements as applicable
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP), Letters of Recommendation (LOR)
  • Apply online via institute’s portal or country’s platform (e.g., UniAssist for Germany)
  • Student visa application and required documents
Major Scholarships
  • Europe: Erasmus Mundus Joint Masters, DAAD Scholarships (Germany), French Government Scholarships, Chevening (UK)
  • Asia: MEXT (Japan), Singapore International Graduate Award (SINGA), Chinese Government Scholarships, Tata Scholarships
  • University and local government scholarships (see institute sites)
Student Life & Tips
  • Campus hostels, student apartments, PGs, or homestays
  • Learn basic local language for daily life (German, French, Japanese, Mandarin, etc.)
  • Join international student networks and clubs
  • Be aware of medical insurance, laws, and emergency contacts
  • Budget for tuition, living costs, and travel
  • Explore city sights, cultural events, and career fairs
Frequently Asked Questions
  • Can I study in Europe for free?
    Some countries like Germany and Norway offer free or low-cost tuition even for international students.
  • Is English education available in Asia?
    Yes, many leading Asian universities have English-taught degrees, especially at Masters/PhD level.
  • What exams are needed?
    Most require IELTS/TOEFL, and some may need GRE/GMAT for specific courses.
  • Can I work during my studies?
    Most European & Asian countries allow part-time campus or off-campus work (hours and visa rules vary).
