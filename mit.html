Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT)

About MIT:

Founded in 1861, MIT is a world-leading private research university located in Cambridge, Massachusetts, USA. Renowned for its innovation and scientific breakthroughs, MIT provides top-ranked programs in science, engineering, technology, business, and humanities. The campus fosters entrepreneurship, global collaborations, and a vibrant student culture.

Established1861

Programs Offered
  • Undergraduate: Computer Science, Engineering (Mechanical, Electrical, Civil, Chemical), Biology, Physics, Mathematics, Architecture, Management, Economics, and more
  • Graduate: Masters/PhD in technical, scientific, business, arts, design, interdisciplinary areas
  • World-leading research programs and labs, entrepreneurship tracks
  • Interdisciplinary and collaborative learning opportunities
Admission & Entry Requirements
  • High school diploma (undergraduate); bachelor's degree (graduate)
  • Academic excellence: strong grades in relevant subjects
  • Standardized tests: SAT/ACT (optional for most years), TOEFL/IELTS (for international applicants)
  • Essays, personal statement, recommendations
  • Application through MIT Admissions portal (Apply here)

Scholarships & Financial Aid
  • MIT offers need-blind admissions for US students and need-based aid for all including international students.
  • Merit-based fellowships at graduate level; department and external scholarships available.
  • Major US awards for international students: Fulbright, private fellowships, national science foundations.
  • Assistive funding: On-campus jobs, teaching/research assistantships.
