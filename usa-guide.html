USA Study Guide

This guide provides application advice, top universities, scholarships, and tips for international students considering higher education in the United States of America.

Why Study in the USA?
  • Home to world-leading universities and research centers
  • Broad academic choices: STEM, arts, business, medicine, and more
  • Strong campus diversity and international communities
  • Flexible degree structures (majors, minors, electives)
  • Advanced technology, research funding, and career opportunities
  • Global recognition of degrees
Top Universities
  • Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT), Stanford University, Harvard University
  • California Institute of Technology (Caltech), University of Chicago, Princeton University
  • University of California (Berkeley, LA, San Diego, etc.), Columbia University, Yale University
  • See full rankings at InstituteInsight Rankings
Eligibility & Application Process
  • Academic records (school/college transcripts, certificates, test scores)
  • Standardized tests:
    • SAT/ACT for undergraduate programs
    • GRE/GMAT for postgraduate/PhD or MBA
    • English proficiency: TOEFL/IELTS
  • Essays/Personal Statement, Letters of Recommendation
  • Apply via university portals or Common App for undergrad
  • Obtain admission, pay SEVIS fee, apply for F-1 student visa
Major Scholarships
  • Fulbright Program (official site)
  • Institutional scholarships (each university offers various awards)
  • International student scholarships (see InstituteInsight Scholarship Listings)
  • Departmental scholarships for STEM, arts, business, etc.
  • See InstituteInsight Scholarship Listings
Student Life & Tips
  • Accommodation: campus dormitories, shared apartments, homestays
  • Participate in student clubs, sports, social activities, volunteering
  • Access campus medical centers and insurance
  • Use public transport, campus shuttle, and student discounts
  • Explore city sights, food, museums, cultural events
  • Average cost: $10,000–18,000/year living; tuition varies by university
Frequently Asked Questions
  • Can I work during my studies?
    Yes, on-campus part-time jobs allowed; internships with appropriate authorization.
  • Is healthcare provided?
    Most universities offer health insurance plans for students.
  • Are scholarships available for international students?
    Yes—through Fulbright, universities, and private organizations.
  • What’s the academic calendar?
    Typically August/September start, May/June end (varies by program).
