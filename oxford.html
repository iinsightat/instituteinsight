University of Oxford

About University of Oxford:

Founded before 1167, Oxford is the oldest university in the English-speaking world. Renowned for its collegiate system, academic excellence, and historic legacy, Oxford is consistently ranked among the top global universities for research, teaching, and innovation.

Establishedbefore 1167
LocationOxford, England, UK
TypePublic Collegiate Research University
Students~24,000
Campus SizeCity-wide, 39 colleges
Contactox.ac.uk, undergraduate.admissions@admin.ox.ac.uk
Programs Offered
  • Undergraduate: Arts, Sciences, Medicine, Engineering, Law, PPE, Humanities, Languages, Social Sciences
  • Graduate: Masters, DPhil (PhD), MBA, advanced research degrees in all major disciplines
  • Colleges include Magdalen, Balliol, St John’s, Christ Church, Lady Margaret Hall, Trinity, and more
Admission & Entry Requirements
  • Undergraduate: UCAS application via chosen Oxford college/school
  • Standardized exams: A-levels, IB, AP, English language exams (IELTS/TOEFL)
  • Interviews, personal statement, entrance tests for many courses, written work
  • Graduate: Application via Oxford portal, research proposal (doctorate), references
  • International: Credential evaluation, English testing, financial proof
