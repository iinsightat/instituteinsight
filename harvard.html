Harvard University

About Harvard University:

Founded in 1636, Harvard University in Cambridge, Massachusetts, is the oldest institution of higher education in the United States and one of the world’s top-ranked research universities, renowned for academic excellence, research, and innovation across disciplines.

Established1636
LocationCambridge, Massachusetts, USA
TypePrivate Research University
Students~23,700 (approx. 7,100 undergrad, 16,600 grad)
Campus Size5,076 acres (multiple campuses)
Contactharvard.edu, college@fas.harvard.edu
Programs Offered
  • Undergraduate: Liberal Arts, Sciences, Engineering, Social Sciences, Humanities, Business, Education
  • Graduate: MA, MSc, MBA, JD, MD, EdD, PhD, master's and doctoral programs in all disciplines
  • Schools: Harvard College, Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, Business, Law, Medicine, Education, Public Health, Government, and more
Admission & Entry Requirements
  • Undergraduate: Application via Common App or Coalition App
  • Standardized tests: SAT/ACT (test-optional for most years), TOEFL/IELTS for international students
  • Academic performance, essays, recommendations, extracurriculars evaluated holistically
  • Graduate: GRE/GMAT/LSAT/MCAT for many programs, online application, interviews
  • International: Academic credential evaluation, English test scores, financial forms
