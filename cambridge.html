University of Cambridge

About University of Cambridge:

Founded in 1209, the University of Cambridge is the world’s fourth-oldest university and a leader in global research and education. Its historic colleges, beautiful campus, and world-changing alumni have made it one of the most highly respected institutions worldwide.

Established1209
LocationCambridge, England, UK
TypePublic Collegiate Research University
Students~20,000
Campus SizeCity-wide, 30+ colleges
Contactcam.ac.uk, admissions@cam.ac.uk
Programs Offered
  • Undergraduate: Arts, Sciences, Engineering, Law, Medicine, Humanities, Social Sciences
  • Graduate: Masters, PhD, MBA, research degrees in all major disciplines
  • Colleges include Trinity, King’s, St. John’s, Magdalene, Clare, Jesus, and more
Admission & Entry Requirements
  • Undergraduate: UCAS application, submitted through chosen Cambridge college
  • Standardized exams: A-levels, IB, APs (SAT/ACT rarely required), English language proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL)
  • Interviews, personal statement, college essays for many subjects
  • Graduate: Application via Cambridge portal, research proposal (for doctorate), recommendations
  • International: Credential evaluation, English test scores, financial documentation
